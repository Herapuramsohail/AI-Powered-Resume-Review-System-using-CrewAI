# ATS Compliance Standards & Best Practices

Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) are software applications used by employers to manage, scan, and sort job applications. To ensure a resume successfully passes through an ATS, it must adhere to strict formatting and structural guidelines.

## 1. Document Structure & File Format
* **Supported Formats**: Always use standard PDF or Microsoft Word (.docx) formats. Avoid using image files (JPEG/PNG) or HTML.
* **Layout Layout**: Use a standard, clean, single-column layout. Multi-column layouts can confuse parsing engines and cause text to be read out of order.
* **Tables and Text Boxes**: Do NOT place important text inside tables or text boxes. ATS parsing systems often strip tables or fail to read content inside floating text boxes.
* **Headers and Footers**: Avoid placing vital contact information in the document header or footer. Some ATS engines skip these areas entirely. Place contact details at the very top of the body text.

## 2. Headings & Section Titles
Use standard, recognizable section headers so the ATS can categorize experience properly. Avoid creative titles.
* **Correct Headers**:
  * "Professional Experience" or "Work History" (Not "Where I've Been" or "My Journey")
  * "Skills" or "Core Competencies" (Not "What I Can Do")
  * "Education" (Not "Schooling")
  * "Projects" or "Technical Projects" (Not "Cool Things I Built")
  * "Certifications" or "Credentials"

## 3. Fonts and Visual Elements
* **Safe Fonts**: Use standard, web-safe fonts such as Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman, Garamond, Georgia, or Helvetica. Avoid custom, downloaded fonts.
* **Graphics and Icons**: Do not include images, charts, logos, diagrams, progress bars, or skill rating circles (e.g., "Python: 4/5 stars"). The parser cannot read them and they may render as corrupt symbols.
* **Hyperlinks**: Keep hyperlinks simple. Use the actual text URL or standard labels. Avoid masking important keywords with links.

## 4. Keyword Density & Matching
* **Semantic Match**: ATS systems search for specific keyword matches from the job description.
* **Exact Phrases**: If the job description asks for "Project Management", do not just write "managing projects". Use the exact noun phrases listed in the posting.
* **Location of Keywords**: Distribute keywords naturally throughout the "Skills", "Summary", and "Experience" sections. Do not "keyword stuff" (listing long blocks of keywords in white text or hidden sections), as recruiters see the plain text output of the parser.
