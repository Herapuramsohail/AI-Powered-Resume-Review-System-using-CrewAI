# Resume Writing Best Practices & Content Optimization

A resume should highlight accomplishments and demonstrate impact. Use these guidelines to write and structure high-impact content.

## 1. The STAR Method for Bullet Points
Each bullet point under your experience should explain the **Situation / Task**, the **Action** you took, and the **Result** you achieved.
* **Bad**: "Responsible for maintaining client websites."
* **Good (STAR)**: "Maintained 15+ client websites, updating codebase to improve load speed by 25% and decrease bounce rate by 8%."

## 2. Incorporate Action Verbs
Start every bullet point with a strong action verb in the correct tense (past tense for previous roles, present tense for current roles). Avoid passive phrases like "assisted with", "responsible for", "helped out", or "worked on".
* **Strong Verbs for Leadership**: Directed, spearheaded, orchestrated, guided, coordinated, mentored, managed.
* **Strong Verbs for Innovation/Creation**: Formulated, engineered, designed, developed, pioneered, implemented, established.
* **Strong Verbs for Efficiency**: Streamlined, optimized, automated, consolidated, reduced, accelerated.

## 3. Quantifiable Accomplishments & Metrics
Always include numbers, percentages, dollar amounts, or timeframes. Metrics provide scale and proof of your abilities.
* **Revenue**: "Generated $50k in new revenue..."
* **Efficiency**: "Reduced deployment time by 40%..."
* **Scale**: "Managed a team of 6 engineers..." or "Maintained a codebase serving 1M+ active users..."
* **Time**: "Delivered project 2 weeks ahead of schedule..."

## 4. Professional Summary Section
* Keep it to 3–4 sentences at the top of the resume.
* Highlight years of experience, core expertise, and a major career achievement.
* Align it with the target job's primary goals.

## 5. Formatting & Cleanliness
* Keep the length to 1 page if under 5–7 years of experience, and a maximum of 2 pages for senior profiles.
* Maintain consistent date formatting (e.g., "MM/YYYY - MM/YYYY" or "Month YYYY - Month YYYY").
* Use standard bullet points rather than creative symbols.
* Ensure margins are between 0.5 to 1 inch on all sides.
